# Progress Note — 2026-04-15

**Patient:** 47-year-old female
**Chief complaint:** Persistent low back pain, 12 weeks, now with intermittent right leg radiation.

## Subjective

Patient is a 47-year-old woman with a 12-week history of mechanical low back pain that began after moving boxes at home. Pain is localized to the lumbosacral region without clear inciting injury. Over the past 4 weeks she has developed intermittent right-sided radiation into the posterior thigh and lateral calf, worse with prolonged sitting and forward flexion, improved with recumbency.

She describes the pain as a 6/10 on average, peaking at 8/10 with activity. She denies any new weakness, numbness in the saddle region, or bowel/bladder changes. She denies fevers, chills, night sweats, unintentional weight loss, history of malignancy, IV drug use, or recent invasive spinal procedure. No significant trauma.

Sleep is disrupted by pain 2-3 nights per week. Work (occupational therapist) is increasingly difficult - she has missed 4 days in the past month.

## Review of conservative therapy (important)

- **Physical therapy:** 8 sessions completed between 2026-02-10 and 2026-03-28 with a licensed PT (McKenzie-directed lumbar extension program + core stabilization). Discharge summary from PT on 2026-03-28 notes "minimal functional improvement; patient compliant with HEP."
- **NSAIDs:** Naproxen 500 mg BID x 6 weeks (2026-02-15 through 2026-03-29). Partial relief only. Discontinued due to dyspepsia.
- **Muscle relaxant:** Cyclobenzaprine 10 mg QHS x 3 weeks (2026-03-01 through 2026-03-22). Helped sleep but did not reduce daytime pain.
- **Activity modification and heat therapy:** ongoing throughout.

Patient has been compliant with all interventions. Despite >6 weeks of appropriate conservative care, symptoms have persisted and developed a radicular component.

## Objective

- **Vitals:** BP 122/78, HR 74, T 36.8 C, BMI 24.1. Afebrile, well-appearing.
- **Neuro:** Alert and oriented. CN II-XII intact. Strength 5/5 bilaterally in all major lower-extremity muscle groups (hip flexion, knee extension, ankle dorsiflexion, EHL, plantarflexion). Sensation intact to light touch in all lumbar dermatomes including S2-S4 (perineal/saddle). Reflexes: patellar 2+ bilaterally, Achilles 2+ bilaterally. Negative Babinski.
- **Back:** Paraspinal tenderness L4-S1. Range of motion limited in forward flexion (fingertips to mid-tibia). Right straight leg raise positive at 60 degrees reproducing posterior thigh pain. Left SLR negative. FABER negative bilaterally. Gait normal, including heel and toe walking.
- **Rectal tone:** normal (documented on focused exam given complaint of radicular symptoms).

## Assessment

1. Chronic low back pain with right lower-extremity radicular symptoms, persistent despite ≥6 weeks of documented conservative therapy (PT, NSAIDs, muscle relaxant).
2. Clinical picture most consistent with L5 or S1 radiculopathy, likely from disc herniation vs. lateral recess stenosis.
3. **No red flags:** denies saddle anesthesia, bowel/bladder dysfunction, fever, weight loss, history of malignancy, IV drug use, recent spinal procedure, or significant trauma. Exam confirms intact saddle sensation, normal rectal tone, no motor deficit.

## Plan

1. **MRI lumbar spine without contrast (CPT 72148)** to evaluate for disc herniation or nerve-root compression now that conservative therapy has failed and radicular symptoms are present without red flags. Non-contrast is appropriate given no history of prior lumbar surgery, no suspicion of infection or malignancy.
2. Continue activity modification. Transition NSAID to topical diclofenac given dyspepsia.
3. Referral to spine surgery if MRI confirms nerve-root compression correlating with symptoms.
4. Follow up in 2 weeks, or sooner if new neurologic deficit, saddle numbness, or bladder/bowel changes develop - patient educated on red-flag symptoms and when to seek emergency care.
