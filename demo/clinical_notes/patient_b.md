# Progress Note — 2026-04-15

**Patient:** 52-year-old male
**Chief complaint:** Low back pain, 10 weeks, requests imaging.

## Subjective

52-year-old man with 10 weeks of axial low back pain that started gradually after a weekend of yard work. Pain is constant, 5-7/10, non-radiating to the lower extremities. No leg numbness, weakness, or paresthesia. No saddle symptoms, no bowel/bladder changes. No night pain. No fever, chills, or weight loss. No history of malignancy. No IV drug use. No recent spinal procedure. No significant trauma beyond the initial strain.

Patient reports the pain is interfering with work (warehouse manager, on feet most of the shift) and with sleep. He is frustrated and specifically requested imaging today - "I want to know what's actually wrong."

### Conservative therapy reported (important - see gap below)

- **NSAIDs:** Ibuprofen 600 mg TID x 5 weeks (2026-03-10 through 2026-04-15). Reports modest relief (pain dropped from 7/10 to 5/10) but plateau.
- **Physical therapy:** Referral placed by this office on **2026-03-22**. Patient attended **one intake evaluation on 2026-03-29** (documented in chart via PT intake note from HealthFirst PT). Patient was **a no-show for the following three scheduled sessions** (2026-04-02, 2026-04-05, 2026-04-09) and has not rescheduled. When asked today, patient stated: *"I didn't feel like it was helping, so I've been doing stretches at home from YouTube instead."*
- **Muscle relaxants:** Not tried.
- **Activity modification:** Partial - patient reports he "tries to lift with his legs" but has not modified his work duties.

## Objective

- **Vitals:** BP 130/82, HR 78, T 36.7 C, BMI 28.4. Afebrile.
- **Neuro:** Alert and oriented. Strength 5/5 throughout lower extremities. Sensation intact to light touch in all lumbar dermatomes including perineal distribution. Patellar and Achilles reflexes 2+ bilaterally. Negative Babinski.
- **Back:** Paraspinal tenderness L3-L5, no midline spinous process tenderness. Forward flexion limited by pain at ~45 degrees. Straight leg raise negative bilaterally. FABER negative. Gait normal. Heel-toe walk normal.

## Assessment

1. Mechanical low back pain, 10 weeks, **without radiculopathy** and **without red flags**.
2. **Incomplete course of conservative therapy.** Patient has documented NSAID trial but has not completed a course of physical therapy (1 intake visit, 3 no-shows, self-reported home exercises of unknown protocol/duration). No documented muscle-relaxant trial.

## Plan

1. **Advanced imaging (MRI lumbar spine without contrast, CPT 72148) requested** at patient's insistence. I recognize the conservative-therapy documentation is incomplete - discussed at length with patient. He declines to re-engage with formal PT and prefers imaging first.
2. Counseled patient extensively that in the absence of red flags or radiculopathy, evidence-based guidelines (ACP, ACR Appropriateness Criteria) recommend 6+ weeks of structured conservative therapy **including supervised PT** before imaging for uncomplicated mechanical LBP. Patient acknowledges but requests we submit the imaging request anyway.
3. Re-referred to PT (HealthFirst PT, Riverside location) with note to the scheduler emphasizing the importance of completing the full course. Provided written home-exercise program as a bridge.
4. Continue ibuprofen. Consider adding cyclobenzaprine QHS if sleep remains disrupted.
5. Follow up in 3 weeks or sooner for any red-flag symptoms. Patient was educated on red-flag symptoms (saddle numbness, leg weakness, bowel/bladder changes, fever) and when to seek emergency care.
