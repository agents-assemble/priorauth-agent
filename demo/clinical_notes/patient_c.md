# Urgent Same-Day Progress Note — 2026-04-15

**Patient:** 61-year-old female
**Chief complaint:** Progressive low back pain, 3 weeks, now with saddle numbness and new difficulty with bladder control. Seen same-day as add-on.

## Subjective

61-year-old woman with past medical history notable for **ER+ invasive ductal carcinoma of the left breast (diagnosed 2019, stage IIA)**, treated with lumpectomy, sentinel node biopsy (negative), and adjuvant radiation. She is on year 7 of adjuvant anastrozole. Last oncology follow-up 2026-01-12 - no evidence of recurrence on that visit.

She presents today with a 3-week history of progressively worsening low back pain that has become severe and **unremitting at night - wakes her from sleep most nights**. Pain radiates bilaterally down both posterior thighs. Oral acetaminophen and ibuprofen have provided no relief.

**Over the past 4 days she has noticed:**

- **Numbness in the perineal and inner-thigh region** ("the area where I'd sit on a bicycle seat feels dull and tingly")
- **Difficulty initiating urination** and, twice in the past 48 hours, **urinary incontinence** ("I couldn't make it to the bathroom")
- **New weakness in both legs** when climbing stairs - she has had to use the handrail, which is new for her
- One fall at home 2 days ago when her right leg "gave out"

She denies fever, chills, recent infection, IV drug use, or spinal procedures. No unintentional weight loss. Constitutional review otherwise unremarkable.

## Objective

- **Vitals:** BP 138/84, HR 88, T 36.9 C, SpO2 98%, BMI 26.1. Patient appears uncomfortable, sitting forward.
- **Neuro:**
  - Strength: hip flexion 4/5 bilaterally, knee extension 4+/5 bilaterally, ankle dorsiflexion 4/5 bilaterally, EHL 4-/5 on the right and 4/5 on the left. **Mild bilateral lower-extremity weakness, right more than left**, new compared to baseline.
  - Sensation: **decreased light touch and pinprick in the S2-S4 distribution bilaterally (saddle/perianal)**. Intact in L2-S1 dermatomes above the saddle.
  - Reflexes: patellar 1+ bilaterally, Achilles absent bilaterally. Babinski negative.
  - **Digital rectal exam: decreased rectal tone**. Patient unable to voluntarily contract the external anal sphincter on command.
  - Post-void residual by bedside bladder scan: **310 mL** (abnormal; overflow incontinence pattern).
- **Back:** Midline tenderness to percussion at L3-L5. Paraspinal tenderness bilaterally. Range of motion severely limited by pain.

## Assessment — URGENT

This presentation is highly concerning for **cauda equina syndrome** in the setting of a patient with a history of breast cancer.

**Red-flag symptoms and signs present:**

1. **Saddle anesthesia** (decreased sensation S2-S4 bilaterally)
2. **Urinary dysfunction** (retention with overflow incontinence; post-void residual 310 mL)
3. **Decreased rectal tone** with loss of voluntary sphincter contraction
4. **Bilateral lower-extremity weakness**, progressive over days, with recent fall
5. **History of malignancy** (ER+ breast cancer, 7 years out; vertebral metastasis must be ruled out - anastrozole does not eliminate this risk)
6. **Night pain unrelieved by rest**, progressive over weeks

Any one of these in isolation warrants urgent imaging. **The combination meets textbook criteria for emergent MRI and likely neurosurgical/spine consultation.**

Standard "failed conservative therapy" criteria are not applicable to this presentation.
